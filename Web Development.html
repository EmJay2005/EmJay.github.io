Web Development

Spiders are fascinating creatures, especially when it comes to their web-making abilities. Here’s a deeper look into how they produce silk, construct webs, and use these marvels of nature to catch prey.

Spiders Produce Silk

Spiders produce silk through specialized glands located in their abdomen. Each gland produces a different type of silk, which can be used for various purposes:

  • searchMajor Ampullate Glands

    Produce dragline silk, which is used for the web’s outer rim and structural parts.

  • searchMinor Ampullate Glands

    Produce auxiliary spiral silk, which helps stabilize the web.

  • searchFlagelliform Glands

    Produce capture spiral silk, which is sticky and used to catch prey.

  • searchAggregate Glands

    Produce glue-like substances used to coat the capture spiral.

  • searchPyriform Glands

    Produce attachment discs to anchor the web to a surface.

  • searchAciniform Glands

    Produce silk used to wrap prey.

Spiders can manipulate their silk properties, making it stretchy, sticky, or tough, depending on their needs.

The Process of Web Construction and the Purposes of Different Web Structures

The web construction process varies among spider species but generally follows these steps:

Frame Construction

The spider builds the outer frame and radii (spokes) of the web.

Auxiliary Spiral

The spider lays down a temporary spiral to keep the radii in place.

Capture Spiral

The spider replaces the auxiliary spiral with a sticky capture spiral that traps prey.

Different web structures serve various purposes:

Orb Webs

Circular and intricate, these are used primarily for catching flying insects.

Sheet Webs

Flat, sheet-like webs that trap crawling insects.

Tangle Webs (Cobwebs)

Irregular webs used to ensnare various types of prey.

Funnel Webs

Funnel-shaped webs with a retreat where the spider hides and waits for prey.

How Spiders Use Their Webs to Catch Prey

Spiders use their webs in several ingenious ways to catch prey:

Vibration Detection

When prey gets entangled, it struggles, sending vibrations through the web that alert the spider.

Sticky Silk

The capture spiral’s sticky silk ensures that prey remains trapped until the spider arrives.

Wrapping Prey

Some spiders quickly wrap their prey in silk to immobilize it, ensuring it doesn’t escape.

Venom Injection

Once the prey is secure, the spider injects venom to paralyze or kill it, then begins the process of digestion.
